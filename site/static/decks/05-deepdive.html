MODULE 5 DEEP DIVE  ·  CONTAINERS FOR GENAI & AGENTIC AI

The knobs nobody explained

The lab wired ChromaDB and Streamlit together with chunk_size=500, k=3, and nomic-embed-text — values that were just already in app/main.py. This deck opens those knobs: chunking, top-k, the similarity metric, and the context budget.

Gourav Shah  ·  School of DevOps & AI  ·  Deep Dive (Part 2)

M5-DD·01

Cut the index card too small and the fact loses its command

A chunk boundary that lands mid-idea splits a fact from the step that goes with it — the model reads one card and gets half a procedure.

A retrieved chunk that's too small hands the model the fact without the command two sentences later — precise, but incomplete.

M5-DD·02

Cut it too large and four procedures blur into one average

An oversized chunk forces the embedding model to compress unrelated procedures into a single vector — the resulting match is mediocre for all of them.

A chunk that swallows several topics forces its embedding to represent the average of all of them — a mediocre match for anything specific.

M5-DD·03

Overlap photocopies the tail onto the next card

The last 50 characters of chunk 1 are repeated at the start of chunk 2 — a fact sitting on the cut boundary is never orphaned with no context on either side.

Overlap exists for facts that land exactly on a chunk boundary — with overlap=50 a boundary fact still appears whole in at least one chunk.

M5-DD·04

This corpus split into exactly 2 chunks, and that's why

The splitter prefers a paragraph break over a mid-sentence cut, trying each separator in order — on the ~1,000-char Acme corpus that means roughly two runbook sections per chunk.

The splitter always prefers a paragraph break over a mid-sentence cut — the chunk count you get is a direct function of corpus size vs chunk_size.

M5-DD·05

Top-k decides how many cards land on the model's desk

k=3 always retrieves the three nearest chunks, regardless of how many exist or how relevant the third-ranked one actually is.

Every retrieved chunk is text the model must read before answering — retrieval doesn't just find information, it spends budget.

M5-DD·06

k=3 requested, 2 returned — you're already retrieving everything

With only 2 chunks total in this collection, k=3 can't filter anything — ChromaDB hands back both and stops; there's no third chunk to return.

On a corpus this small, k isn't doing retrieval work at all; on a larger corpus a k this generous would start diluting more than helping.

M5-DD·07

Distance, not similarity — and lower means closer

ChromaDB's documents collection runs on default squared-L2 (Euclidean) distance — a distance, not a percentage, so smaller numbers mean more relevant.

Every "Found N relevant chunks" line in the lab is ChromaDB returning the smallest-distance chunks — a distance, not a percentage score.

M5-DD·08

Same ranking here, but only because the vectors sit on the unit circle

nomic-embed-text's embeddings are close to unit-norm, and ‖a−b‖² = 2 − 2·cos(a,b) when both vectors have norm 1 — so L2 and cosine agree.

This equivalence is specific to unit-norm vectors — swap in a model that doesn't normalize, and L2 starts rewarding vector length, which cosine ignores.

M5-DD·09

The embedder decides what "similar" means — the LLM never gets a vote

The LLM never sees your documents until after retrieval hands it a slice of text; every retrieval decision is made entirely by the embedding model's vector space.

If the embedding model doesn't distinguish "restart" from "backup" in its vector space, no downstream LLM quality fixes a bad retrieval.

M5-DD·10

Two embedding models never share a coordinate system

Two different models place the same sentence at different, incomparable coordinates — storing embeddings from one and querying with another produces meaningless distances.

Storing embeddings from one model and querying with another doesn't degrade gracefully — it produces meaningless distances, not just worse ones.

M5-DD·11

Four pieces have to fit inside one 4096-token ceiling

num_ctx=4096 caps every prompt this app builds — template scaffolding, retrieved context, the question, and headroom for the answer all compete for the same budget.

num_ctx=4096 is the real ceiling, independent of qwen2.5:1.5b's larger native training context — the app never uses more than this.

M5-DD·12

At this lab's scale, the prompt uses under 15% of the ceiling

Working the real arithmetic for chunk_size=500 and k=3: roughly 350-450 tokens spent, leaving over 3,650 tokens of headroom for the answer.

There's no pressure on this corpus — but raise k to 10 on chunk_size=1500 and context alone hits ~3,750 tokens, crowding out the answer.

M5-DD·13

Ollama keeps the tail and drops the front, verified live

A 33,742-token oversized prompt got cut to prompt_eval_count=2050 with keep=4 — a marker at the front was lost, one at the back survived.

On this app's prompt shape — context first, question last — an over-budget prompt loses your earliest retrieved chunks first, not the question.

M5-DD·14

Two answers wrong for two different reasons

A retrieval miss means the wrong chunk was retrieved; a generation miss means the right chunk was handed over and the model still got it wrong.

Confusing the two wastes debugging time — check "View Source Chunks" or query ChromaDB directly before touching the prompt or the model.

M5-DD·15

Naive RAG never asks "is the 2nd-best chunk actually better?"

There is no re-ranking step between the vector search and the LLM — whatever comes back by raw distance goes straight to the model.

A semantically-adjacent-but-wrong chunk can outrank the truly relevant one — that gap is what M6's agentic approach adds back.

M5-DD·16

ChromaDB's own API confirms the metric, not a guess

Querying /api/v1/collections shows hnsw_configuration.space: "l2" and dimension: 768 straight from the running collection — no override was ever set.

No override was ever set in app/main.py — the collection came up on ChromaDB's default, and the API says so directly.

M5-DD·17

0.6956 and 1.0968 — the numbers behind "Found 2 relevant chunks"

Querying the payments question directly returns two real L2 distances, lower ranked first — only two print because only two chunks exist.

The Streamlit UI never shows you this number — only the resulting text — but this is exactly what ChromaDB ranks on.

M5-DD·18

Three sentences, three vectors, norm 1.000000 every time

Three arbitrary sentences through this app's exact OllamaEmbeddings code path all measure at L2 norm ≈ 1.000000 — confirmed, not assumed.

This confirms the unit-norm claim through the app's exact code path, not a general Ollama guarantee — measured, not assumed.

M5-DD·19

Same corpus, three chunk sizes, three different stories

baseline sits in the middle, variant-a (11 tiny chunks) wins on precision, variant-b (1 giant chunk) loses on every question in this experiment.

variant-a's tiny chunks isolate the exact restart command; variant-b collapses the whole corpus into one chunk every question retrieves.
